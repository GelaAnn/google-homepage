DATABASE DESIGN AND MANAGEMENT

We learn Database Design and Management as a course Database Design and
Management is a course designed for IT students who want to learn how
to design and manage databases.

The course covers the fundamentals of database design, including
entity-relationship modeling, normalization, and database schema design.
Students will learn how to create, modify, and manage databases using SQL.

The course also covers topics such as database security,transaction
management, and performance tuning. Students will gain hands-on experience
through assignments and projects that involve designing and implementing
databases for real-world scenarios.

Upon completion of the course, students will be equipped with the skills
and knowledge necessary to design and manage databases effectively, which
is a crucial skill for many IT positions, such as database administrator,
data analyst, and software developer.

PRINCIPLES OF ACCOUNTING AND MANAGEMENT

As part of our courses we study Accounting and Management
Accounting and Management is a course designed for IT students
to learn financial and business management skills.

The course covers financial accounting, managerial accounting,
financial analysis, budgeting, and strategic planning.
IT students who plan to work in management or start their own
business will benefit from this course.

The course provides a foundation in business and financial
concepts that students can apply to a variety of industries.
Students will gain an understanding of how businesses operate
and how financial decisions impact the success of a company.

The role of technology in accounting and management, including
the use of accounting software and financial analysis tools,
will also be covered. Upon completion of the course, students
will be equipped with the skills and knowledge necessary to
pursue a career in accounting or management in the IT industry.

INTERNET TECHNOLOGIES AND WEB DESIGN

Internet Technologies and Web Design is a course designed for
IT students who want to learn how to create and manage
websites. The course covers a range of topics related to web
design, including HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and responsive design.

Students will learn how to create and publish websites using
content management systems such as WordPress and Drupal.
The course also covers topics such as web hosting, search
engine optimization, and web analytics.

Students will gain hands-on experience through assignments and
projects that involve creating websites for real-world scenarios.

Upon completion of the course, students will be equipped with the
skills and knowledge necessary to create and manage websites
effectively, which is a valuable skill for many IT positions, such
as web developer, web designer, and digital marketing specialist.

SOFTWARE ENGINEERING

Software Engineering is a course designed for IT students to
learn how to design, develop, and maintain software applications.
The course covers the software development life cycle, including
requirements gathering, design, implementation, testing, and
maintenance. Students will learn how to use software development
methodologies such as Agile and Waterfall, and tools such as
version control systems and integrated development environments (IDEs).

The course also covers topics such as software testing, software
quality assurance, and software project management. Students will
gain hands-on experience through assignments and projects that
involve developing software applications for real-world scenarios.

It gives students the opportunity to work with various programming
languages such as Java, Python, and C#.
Upon completion of the course, students will be equipped with the
skills and knowledge necessary to design, develop, and maintain
software applications effectively, which is a crucial skill for
many IT positions, such as software developer, software engineer,
and software project manager.

DATACOMMUNICATIONS AND NETWORKING

Data Communications and Networking is a course designed for IT students
to learn how to design, implement, and manage computer networks.
The course covers topics such as network architecture, network protocols,
network security, and network administration. Students will learn how to
design and configure Local Area Networks (LANs), Wide Area Networks (WANs),
and wireless networks. The course also covers topics such as network
performance optimization, network troubleshooting, and network monitoring.

Students will gain hands-on experience through assignments and projects
that involve designing and configuring computer networks for real-world
scenarios. Students will have the opportunity to work with various network
hardware and software, such as routers, switches, and network management tools.

Upon completion of the course, students will be equipped with the skills and
knowledge necessary to design, implement, and manage computer networks
effectively, which is a valuable skill for many IT positions, such as network
administrator, network engineer, and network security specialist.

INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY LAW

Information Technology Law is a course designed for IT students to learn
about the legal aspects of information technology. The course covers
topics such as intellectual property law, privacy law, data protection
law, cybercrime law, and internet governance.

Students will learn about the legal frameworks that govern information
technology, such as the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the
Electronic Communications Privacy Act (ECPA), and the Computer Fraud and
Abuse Act (CFAA). The course also covers topics such as software licensing,
e-commerce law, and domain name disputes. Students will gain an understanding
of the legal risks and liabilities associated with information technology, and
how to comply with legal requirements in their IT-related work.

Students will have the opportunity to analyze and apply legal concepts to
real-world scenarios. Upon completion of the course, students will be equipped
with the skills and knowledge necessary to navigate the legal landscape of
information technology, which is a valuable skill for many IT positions, such as
IT project manager, IT security specialist, and IT compliance officer.
